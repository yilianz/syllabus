COURSE DESCRIPTION: MATH 141 is an introduction to differential calculus, which presents a mathematical way to describe change and motion.

PREREQUISITES: Grade of C or better in MATH 111 and MATH 112, or placement above MATH 111 and 112.

TEXTS:

  • University Calculus , 4th Edition by Hass, Weir and Thomas

COURSE OBJECTIVES:

  • Learn the concepts of differential calculus, such as limit, derivative, integration, and could communicate using the language of mathematics.
  • Understand mathematical ideas in calculus and could use them in upper level mathematics and other disciplines.
  • Develop basic skills in solving mathematical problems using Maple.

COURSE ORGANIZATION & TOPICS TO BE COVERED: This course is not a traditional mathematical course. Each lecture will be divided into two sections: the instructor's lecture, and the student's exploration and discussion.
Topics include limit, continuity, derivative, applications of the derivative, integrals, fundamental theorem of calculus.
