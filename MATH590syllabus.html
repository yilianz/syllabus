Course Description and Prerequisites

(Prereq: Senior standing in Math/CS or Math Ed major, or consent of Department). Intended primarily for Math/CS Majors and Math Ed Majors. Each student is required to do an in-depth study of some topic to be presented orally and in writing in class, and to participate in the discussion and debate of topics presented by other students. Topics will be tied together under a common theme chosen by the faculty.

Course Objectives

Upon completion of this course and MATH591,

  • Students will be able to identify, plan, and manage a technical project from conception to completion, including a feasibility study.
  • Students will learn to apply principles of the software development lifecycle, including requirements analysis, design, and specification.
  • Students will function effectively on a team, manage project tasks, and collaborate to achieve a common goal.
  • Students will produce clear and comprehensive technical documentation, write professional emails, deliver clear and well-organized presentation, and demonstrate effective interpersonal communication skills among the teams.

Grading Policy

GRADING SCALE:

  • 90-100 A, 87-89 B+
  • 80-86 B, 75-79 C+
  • 68-74 C, 65-67 D+
  • 60-64 D, 0-59 F

Participation(10%): Students are required to attend all group meetings and meetings with an industry/faculty mentor, and submit written/oral feedback as required.

Weekly report/stand-up meeting(10%): Teams are expected to submit a weekly report and each team member will give a short update in weekly stand-up meeting.

Project proposal and review(20%): Each team is required to submit a project proposal, which includes the problem statement, feasibility analysis, project roadmap, tech stack,and so on.

Project grade(25%): This grade is determined by the project progress, completion of project objectives, project management, and team effectiveness.

Individual performance(25%): The grade is based on each student's personal design and implementation contribution, peer review, writing and oral communication assignments, and ethical module.

Final presentation and report(10%): Each team will give a final presentation on the latest product release, issues and technical limitations, and relevant industry standards.

  10. Use of Generative AI Tools in this Course

    Permitted and Encouraged Use:

    • Learning and Tutoring: You may use AI to understand concepts, explain code, and practice coding.
    • Large Projects: For large projects, you are encouraged to use tools like GitHub Copilot for coding and debugging.
    • Attribution: If you use AI tools in an assignment, proper documentation and citation are required.

    Prohibited Use:

    • Simple Programming Assignments and exercises: You may not use AI tools to complete simple programming assignments and in-class exercises.
    • Discussion Forum: You may not use AI tools to generate discussion prompts.
    • Project report and proposal: AI tools can only be used for proof read and grammar check. Generating a substantial part of your report through AI tool is not allowed. For the purpose of grading, students need to be prepared to defense the criticism that the human contribution is limited. Please keep a version history of revision of your word document and cite AI-generated material accurately.
