COURSE DESCRIPTION: A brief introduction, using case studies, to show how applied mathematics is used in government and industry.

PREREQUISITES:  None

COURSE OBJECTIVES: The main objectives of this course is to offer mathematics majors the opportunity to engage in current, real-world applications of applied mathematics.

COURSE EXPECTATIONS: Attendance at each seminar is mandatory for student enrolled. Students are expected to participate in class discussion and in-class activities, review and summarize professional talks and literature, research topics and give effective oral presentations. The students are expected to give two 20-minute presentations for the whole semester.

GRADING POLICY:

  • Participation: 30%
  • Review of technical literatures: 20%
  • In class activities and assignment: 25%
  • Presentation: 25%

GRADING SCALE:

  • 90-100 A, 87-89 B+
  • 80-86 B, 75-79 C+

  • 60-64 D, 0-59 F


Participation: This portion of grade will be based on your consistent attendance at the seminars, and on your participation in the activities for each class. You will be graded on your participation, not on the correctness of your answers.
