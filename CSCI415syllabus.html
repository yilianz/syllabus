COURSE DESCRIPTION:  The study of concepts and components in data communications and computer networks. Topics will include data transmission, reference models, common network protocols, network applications, and technologies.

PREREQUISITES:  Grade of C or better in CSCI220.

TEXTBOOK &Equipment:

  • Computer Networking -- A Top-Down Approach,  7th Ed by Kurose and Ross.

COURSE OBJECTIVES:

This is a one-semester introductory course on computer networking. The goal of this course is breadth, not depth to cover the subjects. Only selected topics will be taught and students are expected to read extensively outside of the class. This course is a foundation course for students who are looking for a career path in IT support and network administrator. Writing communication, especially technical writing is essential in the professional working environment. At the end of the class, students will
  • have a fundamental knowledge of how computer network and internet operate.
  • have a good understanding of network layers and common Internet protocols, such as TCP/IP, UDP, ICMP and etc.
  • be able to develop simple, yet practical network applications.
  • be able to troubleshoot networking problems and present the solutions clearly.
